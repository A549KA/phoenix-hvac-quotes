Best AC Thermostat Settings for Phoenix Summer 2026: Save Money & Stay Cool

Every Phoenix homeowner knows the struggle: summer electric bills that rival a mortgage payment. But what if you could stay comfortable AND save hundreds of dollars a year just by changing a few numbers on your thermostat?

The ideal thermostat setting for Phoenix summer is 78°F when you're home and 85°F when you're away. But there's more to it than that — where you set it, when you adjust it, and what type of thermostat you use all make a difference.

Why 78°F Is the Magic Number for Phoenix

Energy Star and SRP (Salt River Project) both recommend 78°F as the optimal thermostat setting for cooling season in Arizona. Here's why:

  • Each degree below 78°F adds 3–5% to your cooling costs. Setting your thermostat to 74°F instead of 78°F can increase your electric bill by $40–$80 per month.
  • At 78°F with ceiling fans, it feels like 72°F. The wind-chill effect from a fan makes you feel cooler — without the AC having to work harder.
  • Your AC system runs more efficiently. The less the system has to run, the longer it lasts. Short cycling from aggressive temperature settings wears out compressors faster.
💡 Tip: Use ceiling fans counterclockwise in summer to create a downdraft. This can make the room feel 4–6°F cooler without touching the thermostat.

Smart Thermostats: The Phoenix Homeowner's Best Investment

If you don't have a smart thermostat yet, you're leaving money on the table. A smart thermostat like the Nest or Ecobee can save the average Phoenix home $150–$250 per year on cooling costs.

Key smart thermostat features for Phoenix:

  • Auto-away scheduling — Automatically raises the temperature when you leave the house.
  • Geofencing — Uses your phone's location to start cooling as you head home.
  • Peak-cooling alerts — Some models communicate with SRP or APS to reduce usage during peak demand.
  • Humidity monitoring — In Phoenix, controlling humidity (surprisingly important in the desert) improves comfort.
  • Remote control — Change your thermostat from anywhere via app.

Does Turning the AC Off During the Day Save Money?

Yes — but with a catch. In Phoenix, turning your AC completely off when you're at work can backfire. Here's why:

When it's 110°F outside and your home heats up to 90°F+ inside, your AC has to run continuously for 2–4 hours just to pull the temperature back down to 78°F. That surge uses more energy than maintaining a steady temperature.

The sweet spot: Set your thermostat to 82–85°F while you're away, not OFF. This gives your AC a reasonable temperature to recover from — typically 30–45 minutes instead of hours.

Nighttime AC Settings for Better Sleep

The ideal sleeping temperature is 65–70°F, but in Phoenix, running your AC that low all night is expensive. Instead:

  • Set your thermostat to 76–77°F at night.
  • Use a ceiling fan (counterclockwise) in the bedroom for extra cooling.
  • Consider a bedroom-specific AC unit or portable AC for smaller homes.
  • Close blinds and curtains during the day to keep heat out.

The best approach is to program your thermostat to cool to 76°F about 30 minutes before bedtime and return to 78°F about 30 minutes before you wake up.

Other Ways to Lower Your Phoenix Cooling Bill

  • Block the sun: Close blinds, curtains, and reflective shades during peak sun hours. This can reduce indoor temperature by 5–10°F.
  • Use a programmable schedule: Don't manually adjust — set it and forget it with a smart or programmable thermostat.
  • Seal air leaks: Check windows, doors, and ductwork for leaks. Phoenix homes lose a lot of cooled air through poor sealing.
  • Replace air filters monthly: A dirty filter makes your AC work 10–15% harder. During summer, check filters every 3–4 weeks.
  • Schedule annual maintenance: A $100–$150 spring tune-up can prevent summer breakdowns and keep your system running at peak efficiency.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What should I set my AC to in Phoenix summer?

78°F when home, 76°F for sleeping, and 82–85°F when away. This balances comfort with energy savings.

Q: Does turning the AC up during the day save money?

Yes, raising the temperature by 5–7°F when you're away can save 10–15% on cooling costs. But don't turn it completely off, or recovery time will waste energy.

Q: What is the best smart thermostat for Phoenix?

Nest Learning Thermostat and Ecobee SmartThermostat with voice control are both excellent choices. Ecobee has a remote sensor option that's great for larger Phoenix homes.

Q: Is 72°F too cold for AC in Phoenix?

72°F is very expensive in Phoenix summers. At 72°F, your AC runs nearly constantly, which can increase your electric bill by 40–60% compared to 78°F.

Q: How much does a smart thermostat cost installed in Phoenix?

A smart thermostat costs $150–$350 for the unit plus $100–$200 for professional installation. Total: $250–$550. Payback period is typically 1–2 years through energy savings.

Final Thoughts

Your thermostat settings have a huge impact on both comfort and cost during Phoenix summers. The magic formula: 78°F at home, ceiling fans on, a smart thermostat running on a schedule. You'll sleep better, save money, and extend the life of your AC system.
